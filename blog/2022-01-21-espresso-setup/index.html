The Simplest Espresso

Jan 2022 - craft life

Espresso is a meditative experience. It’s as much about understanding fluid dynamics as practicing precise handiwork.

Current Setup

I use the Flair Signature and a Timemore C2 modded to have 24 clicks per rotation instead of 12. The mod: I used a power drill to put dimples into the grind size selector between the existing settings. Altogether the setup is about $200, depending on how the machines are sourced. I use a regular kitchen scale.

The grinder isn’t optimal, but I’ve gotten close to dialing it in. The “standard espresso” range tends to be 7 - 8.5 clicks. A lot of the work in dialing in comes from modifying dosage (11g-15g) and tamp technique (light, heavy, or with nutation).

Helpful Tricks

  1. My kitchen scale doesn’t fit well under the Flair, so I’ve set up a simple formula on Google Sheets that calculates how much water should be going into the chamber to get a given output.
  2. Steam preheat is a really the most effective. I use a steel sink strainer.
  3. Really taking care with my WDT (paperclip) tends to make a big difference in channeling.
  4. To make clean up easier, I dry out the chamber after waiting a bit by pressing into a small bowl - from there it’s very easy and clean to blow out the puck.
  5. Another cleanup trick with the pressure gauge is to push the piston section back out by placing the chamber upside down in the sink, and using the plastic tamp.
