Playing with gifs

May 11, 2017 - art

Ever since I first saw a cinemagraph on Reddit, I’ve wanted to make one.

The stars aligned: when I visited Lamu, I actually remembered. With possible cinemagraphs in mind, I took a bunch of pictures in continuous shooting.

Realizing that creating a full cinemagraph would be a decent amount of work, I first threw my pictures onto Google photos, which has an automatic animation utility in Assistant. This would give a good preview for how they might look. Here’s how they turned out (slow loading!):








They came out better than expected — some of the gifs are a bit choppy, and my suspicion is it’s related to my shooting speed (the M10 has 2 speeds). The one with the old, rusted motorboat seems to look best — and has the most potential for telling a story. It will be paired with a handdrawn animation. I have tried a simple one before:



At this point I dearly, dearly regretted not taking cinemagraph photos in Munich and Copenhagen as well.

Next steps are to actually make the cinemagraph. Conveniently enough, the sidebar on the r/cinemagraph subreddit already had a walkthrough video:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2DU0Sim_BJ4&index=3&list=FLeQ2k5Fm3gR2922N4OzlMVQ

Goal: make it better than the one autogenerated by Google.
